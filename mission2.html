Mission 2: The long game & The fresh start

In the mission "The Long Game," the Assassin is tasked with infiltrating a rival gang's hideout by the river to steal a valuable shipment for Ned Wynert. This mission highlights Wynert's involvement in criminal activities and shows the River Thames as a key location for illegal trade and gang battles in Victorian London.
In the mission "A Fresh Start," the Assassin and Wynert make a dramatic escape by boat on the River Thames. The player helps them evade capture by authorities and rival gangs, leading to an exciting climax where they jump their boat over a tall bridge to escape. This final, desperate move on the Thames allows them to break free and start anew.

Historical records show that gangs often targeted cargo being transported on the River Thames. In 1844, it was reported that more than £500,000 worth of goods were stolen from ships and wharves on the Thames each year [1]. The presence of valuable goods, lack of security, and easy boat escapes made the river an appealing target for criminals. Smuggling was also a major issue, with gangs using the Thames to move illegal goods. In 1848, a Parliamentary report estimated that over £14 million worth of contraband was smuggled through the Port of London annually [2]. To combat the rise in crime, the world's first police force dedicated to a specific area, the Thames River Police, was established in 1800 [3]. Initially, there were only eight officers who patrolled the river to stop theft, smuggling, and other crimes [4]. By the mid-19th century, the force had grown to about 150 officers [5]. However, corruption soon took hold, with some officers reportedly working with the very criminals they were supposed to arrest, taking bribes and protecting certain gangs [6].
